Classical Music

Early Roots

Spanning all the way back from the as early as the ninth century, Classical music is a style of music that can be charazzterized with its formality and complexity. It also uses lots of polyphony which is the use of many instruments or tones at once. As it began it was mostly used in religious settings and was primarily centered in Europe.







Baroque Period (1580-1750)

The Baroque period brought about some more common stardards for classical music. For instance the size of orchesetras grew and this led to new ways of expressing this style. In Italy opera was created and along with that forms such as sonatas and concertos started to be used. This meant that the genre was moving towards more complexity instead of simpler songs. Some composers of this era were Johan Sebastian Bach, Antonio Vivaldi and George Frideric Handel.







The clasical Period (1730-1820)

Many people characterize this period as absolute music. Much of this time was perfrormed in full symphonies, string quartets and concertos.
